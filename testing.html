Testing


The main goal of this product is to have a mobile app that allows people to have reference material readily avaiable to them through a database provided by our Client. along with that we will allow the user to log in and fill out a lot of information that will be saved to the database. The information they collect will be about the information about the accident they're investigating. Everything besides the reference material pages is not on the lite version of the app, but on the full version.

Vehicle Search

  • Clicking on the year will bring you to a screen where you select the year and selecting the year will bring you back to the original page and will hold the value of year that was chosen
  • After the selection for the year is done you can type in the vehicle make as long its a valid make then you can press search and it will bring you to the next page, otherwise it will tell you to input a correct make
  • When you select a model it will let you choose which type of model and hold the value. Then when you press next it will bring you to the next page to pick the trim
  • When selecting the trim you pick a value from the picker and the value will hold in it. Then when you press get vehicle info it will bring the values that are in the database for what has been inputted and provide the user with information

EDR Speed at Impact Calculator

  • Hit the switch at the top of the page and it will turn it on and allow the user to input into the slip percentage breaks
  • The values entered into the text boxes hold value when they're inputted
  • After every space is filled up you can press calculate and it will bring you to the calulator results page that will provide the user with a bunch of information about what was calculated

Coefficient of Friction on Various Roadway Surfaces

  • All the values that are chosen in the pickers hold value.

System Testing

Tools/Language used

The language that was used in coding this app was React Native. The tools that were used were Android Studio and Sublime to write our code in and the Emulator was through Android studio to emulate the phone.

Android Testing

  • This has been throughly tested through an android emulation app. All the functionality of the app besides the camera works fine on an android phone.
  • It has been tested on multiple different types of android phones through emulation and functions perfectly fine.
  • We have run the app on our own android phones, not through the emulator and it functions exactly how it does during the emulation.

IOS Testing

  • Tested on several different I-phones. All of the functionality of the app works on the iphone. The reference material pages that we pushed to the app store all work as intended and there are no diffrences in performance compared to Android

Customer Testing (user-based)

The customer testing will be handled most likely by our client when he shows it off at the conference he is going to in May. He wants to unveil what all three groups have been working on at the conference and possibly show other people who're interested in the progress it has made and potentially what is in store for future plans.

Types of possible tests

  • Testing if the UI that has been made is easy to follow and everyone can understand it.
  • Testing if there is complete functionality within the fields of the app. Such fields include text inputs, drop down menus and navigation.
  • Ensuring that there is possibility for future expansion on the project.
  • Making sure the database functionality works completely fine across the mobile and desktop application.
